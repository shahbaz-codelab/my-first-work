BMW M5 COMPETITIONS

The BMW M3 GTR is a high-performance variant of the M3 series, specifically the E46 generation produced in the early 2000s. It was designed primarily for racing, particularly for the American Le Mans Series (ALMS). The M3 GTR is notable for its 4.0-liter V8 engine, which was a significant departure from the inline-six engines found in other M3 models of that era. This V8 engine produced around 493 horsepower in the racing version, contributing to its impressive performance on the track. Due to its success in racing, the road-going version of the M3 GTR is highly sought after by collectors, though it is rare, with only a few units made for homologation purposes.

BMW e36

The BMW E36, produced from 1990 to 2000, is the third generation of the 3 Series. Known for its sleek design, driving dynamics, and variety of body styles, it remains popular among enthusiasts. Design and Exterior: The E36 features a sleek, aerodynamic design with a long hood, short overhangs, and signature BMW elements like the kidney grille and dual headlights.

bmw s1000 rr

The BMW S1000RR is a high-performance superbike with a 999cc inline 4-cylinder engine producing 205.51 hp and 113 Nm of torque. It has a top speed of over 300 km/h and can accelerate from 0-100 km/h in about 3 seconds. Weighing 197 kg (fully fueled), it features advanced electronics like Dynamic Traction Control (DTC), cornering ABS, quickshifter, and riding modes (Rain, Road, Dynamic, Race).
